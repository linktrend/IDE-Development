# Confidence Thresholds

## Purpose

Confidence is a discipline tool, not a performance claim.

Use it to decide whether to keep questioning or move into `INTENT.md`.

## Rule

Confidence is not evidence.

A high number is meaningless if you still cannot explain:

- who the work is for
- why it matters
- what success means
- what constraints are binding

## Practical Thresholds

- below `70%`: discovery is still active; important uncertainties remain
- `70%` to `94%`: intent may be emerging, but questioning should continue if important ambiguity remains
- around `95%`: stop questioning when the remaining uncertainty is unlikely to change intent, scope, or constraints materially

## How To Use The Number

After each answer:

1. restate your best current hypothesis
2. update the confidence estimate
3. name what is still unresolved if confidence is not yet high enough

## When To Stop

Stop discovery when:

- additional questions are producing diminishing returns
- the remaining uncertainty is minor
- `INTENT.md` can be written without silent assumption-filling

## When Not To Pretend You Are Done

Do not stop simply because:

- the user seems impatient
- you want to get to planning faster
- the idea sounds familiar
- you have enough to invent a plausible solution
