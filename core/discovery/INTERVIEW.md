# Interview

## Purpose

Interview is the interactive part of discovery.

Its job is to extract what the user actually wants before planning or structure starts to harden around bad assumptions.

## Preconditions

Interview requires:

- a live, responsive user
- a materially ambiguous or greenfield ask

If the user is not available and the ask is under-specified, stop and treat that as a blocker instead of guessing.

## Interview Process

1. Write a one-sentence current hypothesis of what the user wants.
2. Write an honest confidence estimate.
3. Ask one focused question at a time.
4. Attach your current best guess when useful to surface your assumptions.
5. Wait for the answer before asking the next question.
6. Update the hypothesis and confidence after each answer.
7. Stop when confidence is high enough to write `INTENT.md` responsibly.

## What To Surface

Discovery should reduce uncertainty around:

- who the work is for
- why it matters now
- what success looks like
- what is explicitly in scope
- what is explicitly out of scope
- what constraints are binding

## What Interview Must Not Do

- do not turn into planning
- do not decompose into modules or issues
- do not batch many questions at once
- do not widen the problem unnecessarily with option lists
- do not invent constraints the user has not confirmed

## Stop Condition

Interview stops when uncertainty is low enough that `INTENT.md` can be written without silent guesswork.

Use `CONFIDENCE-THRESHOLDS.md` to judge this.

## Output

The output of interview is not a standalone execution artifact.

The output is:

- clarified objective
- clarified scope
- clarified constraints
- enough confidence to write `INTENT.md`
